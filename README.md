 Existing studies on remaining useful life (RUL) prediction for lithium-ion batteries mainly focus on point estimation, which makes it difficult to effectively characterize the uncertainty and distributional properties of the prediction results. To address this issue, this paper proposes an interval prediction method that integrates an empirical degradation model with a data-driven model, termed DEDM-IFSCN-KDE, for high-accuracy RUL prediction and uncertainty quantification of lithium-ion batteries. First, according to the dynamic variations of feature parameters such as voltage and current during the cyclic charging process, ten health indicators capable of characterizing capacity degradation are extracted, and five highly correlated health indicators are selected as model inputs using the Pearson correlation coefficient. Next, a double exponential degradation model (DEDM) is employed to characterize the long-term degradation trend of battery capacity. Furthermore, fuzzy inference, Cauchy mutation, an adaptive activation function selection strategy, and Laplacian regularization are incorporated into the stochastic configuration network (SCN) to develop an improved fuzzy stochastic configuration network (IFSCN). The proposed IFSCN is then employed to learn and compensate for the prediction residuals of the DEDM, thereby improving the accuracy of capacity degradation prediction. On this basis, the point prediction of RUL is determined according to the intersection between the predicted capacity trajectory and the failure threshold. Finally, kernel density estimation (KDE) is adopted to construct the confidence interval of RUL prediction, thereby enabling effective uncertainty quantification. Experimental results on eight datasets of different types of lithium-ion batteries from NASA and CALCE demonstrate that, at the 95% confidence level, the proposed DEDM-IFSCN-KDE model achieves a high prediction interval coverage probability and a small mean prediction interval width. Compared with single-model methods, it provides a better tradeoff between prediction accuracy and interval compactness, thereby verifying its effectiveness and superiority in lithium-ion battery RUL interval prediction.
